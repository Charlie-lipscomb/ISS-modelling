Columbus Laboratory Module

The Columbus Laboratory is Europe’s main contribution to the International Space Station. Built by the European Space Agency, Columbus was launched aboard Space Shuttle Atlantis in February 2008. It is a state-of-the-art research facility that allows astronauts to perform experiments in life sciences, fluid physics, materials science, fundamental physics, and technology development. Columbus also supports experiments outside the module, facing Earth and space for advanced observation and monitoring.
