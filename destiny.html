Destiny Laboratory Module

The Destiny Lab is the primary U.S. research module aboard the International Space Station. It was launched in February 2001 and provides space for 24 research and system racks. Inside, astronauts conduct cutting-edge experiments in biology, physics, Earth science, and technology development. Destiny has a large viewing window and plays a crucial role in advancing science that benefits both life on Earth and future missions beyond low-Earth orbit.
