Zvezda Service Module

Zvezda (“Star” in Russian) is the core of the Russian segment of the International Space Station. Launched in 2000, it provides living quarters, life support systems, and essential station functions such as propulsion and guidance. Zvezda houses sleeping stations, a galley, and hygiene facilities, as well as mission-critical systems like oxygen generation and communications. It remains one of the most vital modules for sustaining long-duration human presence in orbit.
