Harmony Node (Node 2)

Harmony, also known as Node 2, is the central connecting module of the International Space Station. Launched in 2007, it links the U.S. laboratory Destiny, the European Columbus laboratory, and the Japanese Kibo laboratory. Harmony also provides living quarters for crew, including sleeping areas and life support systems. It distributes power and data throughout the station, making it a key utility hub. Harmony’s ports also allow visiting spacecraft such as SpaceX Dragon and Cygnus to dock, supporting cargo and crew operations.
