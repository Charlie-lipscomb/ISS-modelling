Zarya (Functional Cargo Block)

Zarya, also known as the Functional Cargo Block (FGB), was the very first module of the International Space Station, launched in 1998. Built by Russia and funded by the United States, it provided early power, propulsion, and storage for the growing station before other modules arrived. Today, Zarya still serves as a storage facility and pressurised cargo hold, though many of its original systems have been replaced by newer modules.
