Assembly of the International Space Station

The ISS was not launched all at once, but assembled in orbit through dozens of missions from 1998 onward. Each module and component was delivered by Space Shuttle, Proton, or Soyuz rockets and carefully connected in space. This step-by-step construction, carried out by crews from many nations, represents one of the greatest engineering achievements in history.
