The International Space Station

The International Space Station (ISS) is the largest human-made structure in orbit, launched in 1998 through cooperation between the United States, Russia, Europe, Japan, and Canada. It serves as a world-class laboratory for science, engineering, and international collaboration, hosting continuous crews since 2000. Astronauts aboard conduct research in biology, physics, Earth observation, and technology that benefits life on Earth and prepares humanity for deeper space missions.
