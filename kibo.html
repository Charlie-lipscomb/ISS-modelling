Kibo Laboratory Module

The Japanese Experiment Module, known as Kibo (“hope”), is Japan’s main contribution to the International Space Station. It is the station’s largest single module and provides a versatile environment for research in life sciences, space medicine, material sciences, and technology. Kibo includes a pressurised lab, an unpressurised exposed facility for experiments directly exposed to space, and its own robotic arm to position payloads. This makes Kibo one of the most advanced and flexible research modules aboard the ISS.
