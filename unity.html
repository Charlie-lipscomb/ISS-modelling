Unity Node (Node 1)

Unity was the first U.S. module launched for the International Space Station in 1998. Also known as Node 1, Unity serves as a connecting hub that links together multiple parts of the station. With six docking ports, it provides passageways to the Russian segment, U.S. laboratory modules, airlocks, and visiting spacecraft. Unity’s role is essential for the station’s structure and functionality, as it distributes power, data, and environmental control systems between connected modules.
