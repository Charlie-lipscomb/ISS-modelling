The Cupola

The Cupola is the observation module of the International Space Station, attached to the Tranquility node. Launched in 2010, it features seven windows that provide panoramic views of Earth and space. Astronauts use the Cupola to monitor spacecraft arrivals and departures, operate the station’s robotic arm, and take breathtaking photos of our planet. It has become a favourite place on the ISS for astronauts to look down on Earth and reflect on the fragility of our world.
